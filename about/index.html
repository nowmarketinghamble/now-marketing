Our story

Built to do things differently.

Now Marketing was founded in 2003 by Mike Lewis with a straightforward idea: that clients deserve direct access to the people actually doing the work. No account managers acting as a telephone between you and the creatives. No junior teams picking up senior-billed projects. Just thoughtful, experienced creative people who genuinely care about the results.

From day one, the agency was built to be full-service. Brand identity, print design, web, digital marketing, copywriting, exhibitions and signage -- all under one roof, all informed by the same creative thinking. That breadth is not accidental. Marketing works best when the brand, the message and the medium are joined up, and that only happens when the same team is thinking about all of them together.

Based at The Creative Hub in Netley Abbey, Southampton, we work with clients across Hampshire, Dorset, Wiltshire, West Sussex and beyond. Some have been with us for over a decade. That kind of longevity only happens when you consistently deliver work that makes a genuine difference to a client's business.

We are not the biggest agency in the South. That is deliberate. Staying at a size where Mike is personally involved in every project means our clients always get our best thinking, our best creative and our best attention. It also means that when you brief us, you are briefing the person who will actually design, write and deliver the work.

Over twenty years, we have built brands from scratch, relaunched businesses going through change, designed hundreds of print campaigns, built websites that convert, and helped clients at every stage of growth. The work is always different. The standard never is.

What we believe

Three principles that guide everything.

01
Honesty over jargon

We tell you what your project actually needs, not what sounds impressive in a credentials deck. If a simpler solution will serve you better, we will say so. If your brief has a problem, we will tell you before we start, not after. Good creative partnerships are built on candour, and we would rather have a difficult conversation early than a costly one later.

02
Delivery over promises

Every project we take on has a clear timeline and a clear budget. We stick to both. Agencies that are brilliant at pitching but inconsistent at delivery are frustrating to work with, and we have never built our reputation that way. When we say something will be ready, it will be ready. When we say something will cost a certain amount, it will cost that amount.

03
Partnership over transactions

We do not think of client relationships as a series of invoices. We invest time in understanding your business, your market and your ambitions so that every piece of work we produce is pulling in the right direction. Many of our clients have been with us for five, ten or fifteen years. That only happens when an agency genuinely treats success as a shared objective.

Founder and Creative Director

Mike Lewis

Mike Lewis founded Now Marketing in 2003 after more than a decade working in design and brand agencies across the South of England. His background spans corporate identity, print design, web and digital -- a breadth that made a genuinely full-service agency the obvious model when he set out on his own.

What makes Mike's approach distinctive is his personal involvement in every project the agency takes on. Where many agency founders move into a management role as their business grows, Mike has deliberately kept the agency at a scale where he remains the lead creative on every brief. That means clients are working directly with a designer and strategist with over twenty years of experience, not being handed to someone more junior once the relationship is established.

Based at The Creative Hub in Netley Abbey, Southampton, Mike works across brand, web, digital, print and exhibitions for clients throughout Hampshire and the wider South. He is particularly experienced in property marketing, having worked with some of the region's most respected estate agents and residential developers over many years.

When Mike is not working on client projects, he spends his time thinking about how brands communicate -- what makes some brands feel compelling and others forgettable, and how to close that gap for the businesses he works with.
