Brands built to last.

A brand is not just a logo. It is the entire way your business presents itself to the world: the language you use, the colours you choose, the way your communications feel in someone's hands or on their screen. When all of those elements are working together consistently, they create something that is greater than any individual part -- a brand that people recognise, trust and choose.

At Now Marketing, we have been building brands for businesses in Southampton, Hampshire, Dorset, Wiltshire and West Sussex for over twenty years. Our approach starts with strategy. Before we draw a single line or select a colour, we spend time understanding your business, your competitors and the audience you are trying to reach. That strategic foundation is what ensures the creative work we produce actually positions you correctly in your market, not just looks good.

From there, we develop your visual identity: the logo, colour palette, typography and secondary design elements that will form the building blocks of your brand. We work through concepts carefully and collaboratively, explaining our thinking at every stage so that the final result feels like yours rather than ours.

Every branding project concludes with a comprehensive set of brand guidelines -- a practical reference document that ensures your brand is applied consistently whether you are producing a business card, building a website, briefing a printer or briefing a social media team. Guidelines are not bureaucracy; they are insurance.

We have created and refreshed brands for businesses at every stage of growth, from new ventures establishing their identity for the first time to established businesses that have outgrown what they started with. Whatever the starting point, the objective is always the same: a brand that positions you compellingly and serves you well for years to come.

Our process

Four steps to a stronger brand.

01
Research

We immerse ourselves in your business, your sector and your competitive landscape. What are your competitors doing? What do your customers actually respond to? Where is the white space? Research shapes everything that follows.

02
Concept

We develop a small number of distinct brand directions rather than a large number of similar ones. Each concept is presented with the strategic rationale that sits behind it, so you can make an informed choice rather than a gut reaction.

03
Refine

We develop the chosen direction to completion: fine-tuning the logo, developing the supporting identity elements, building out the colour system and typography, and exploring how the brand looks and feels across different applications.

04
Deliver

Final logo files in every format you will ever need, a full brand guidelines document, stationery design and any additional brand collateral agreed as part of the project scope. Clear, organised and ready to use immediately.

Why it matters

The case for brand investment.

Brand investment is often treated as a luxury -- something businesses get round to once everything else is in place. That thinking is the wrong way round. Your brand is the first thing potential customers see before they know anything else about you, and in most markets the time they spend making a first judgement is measured in seconds rather than minutes. A brand that communicates quality, clarity and relevance immediately will always outperform one that makes people work harder to understand what you stand for.

The return on brand investment is real but often indirect, which makes it easy to undervalue. Businesses with strong brands charge more for the same products and services because perceived quality is higher. They convert enquiries more reliably because the credibility signals are already in place. They retain clients longer because brand consistency creates a feeling of stability and trustworthiness. And they attract better talent, because good people want to work for organisations that take themselves seriously.

In highly competitive markets -- property, professional services, luxury goods -- a strong brand is often the single most important differentiator available to a business. When the products or services are broadly comparable, how you present yourself determines who gets chosen. A well-crafted, professionally executed brand is a competitive advantage that keeps working long after the investment is made.

The cost of a good rebrand, spread over the years it serves you, is rarely as significant as it appears at the time. The cost of a weak or inconsistent brand -- in lost credibility, lost enquiries and the need to redo the work eventually anyway -- is almost always higher.

We work with businesses in Southampton, Hampshire, Dorset, Wiltshire and West Sussex to build brands that are worth investing in: distinctive, considered, and built to last.
